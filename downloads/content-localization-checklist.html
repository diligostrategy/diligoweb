Content Localization Checklist

20 essential checkpoints for culturally accurate, SEO-optimized localized content

Published by Diligo Strategy · 2025 Edition · diligostrategy.com

Use this checklist for every piece of content you localize. Print it, check off each item, and ensure nothing falls through the cracks. Each point includes a brief explanation of why it matters and what action to take.

Cultural Adaptation
1

Cultural references are localized

Replace idioms, metaphors, humor, and cultural references with locally relevant equivalents. A sports analogy that works in the US (baseball) should become football in Europe or cricket in India.

Why: Untranslatable references confuse readers and signal that the content was not written for them.

Action: Flag all culture-specific references for native reviewer
2

Images and visuals are culturally appropriate

Review all images, icons, and graphics for cultural sensitivity. Stock photos should reflect the ethnic diversity of the target market. Hand gestures, colors, and symbols carry different meanings across cultures.

Why: Visual mismatches undermine credibility and can cause offense in some markets.

Action: Source region-appropriate imagery for each market
3

Color symbolism is verified

White symbolizes purity in Western cultures but mourning in parts of East Asia. Red means luck in China but danger in the West. Verify that your color palette does not carry unintended connotations.

Why: Color associations are deeply ingrained and can subconsciously affect brand perception.

Action: Document color meanings per target market
Formatting & Standards
4

Date formats follow local conventions

US uses MM/DD/YYYY, most of Europe uses DD/MM/YYYY, Japan uses YYYY/MM/DD. Ambiguous dates like 03/04/2025 will be misread in half your markets.

Why: Incorrect date formats cause confusion and erode trust in data-heavy content.

Action: Set locale-specific date format rules in your CMS or style guide
5

Number and currency formats are correct

Decimal separators (period vs. comma), thousands separators, and currency symbols vary by region. 1,000.50 in the US is 1.000,50 in Germany. Always use the local currency or clearly mark USD/EUR.

Why: Incorrect number formats can completely change the meaning of financial data.

Action: Apply locale-aware number formatting consistently
6

Measurement units are localized

Convert miles to kilometers, Fahrenheit to Celsius, pounds to kilograms, and inches to centimeters for metric-system markets. Provide dual units where precision matters.

Why: Unfamiliar units force readers to do mental math, increasing cognitive load and bounce rates.

Action: Create a conversion reference sheet for your localization team
7

Address and phone number formats are correct

Address structures vary widely: postal code before or after city, state vs. province, floor/building conventions. Phone numbers need country codes and local formatting.

Why: Incorrect contact formats reduce conversion on contact pages and landing pages.

Action: Validate all contact information with local format standards
Legal & Compliance
8

Privacy and data collection comply with local laws

GDPR in Europe, LGPD in Brazil, PIPA in South Korea, PIPL in China. Each has different consent requirements, data storage rules, and user rights. Cookie banners, privacy policies, and opt-in mechanisms must be localized.

Why: Non-compliance can result in significant fines and legal liability.

Action: Have local legal counsel review each market's compliance requirements
9

Disclaimers and legal text are locally valid

Terms of service, disclaimers, and warranty text must be legally valid in the target jurisdiction. Direct translations of US legal language may not hold up in other legal systems.

Why: Invalid legal text provides no protection and may create liability.

Action: Commission local legal review for all regulatory content
10

Industry-specific regulations are addressed

Financial services, healthcare, food, and pharmaceutical content face additional regulations in each market. Claims that are allowed in one country may be prohibited in another.

Why: Regulated industries face heightened scrutiny and penalties for non-compliant content.

Action: Map regulatory requirements per industry per market before localization
Tone & Language Adaptation
11

Formality level matches market expectations

Japanese business communication requires keigo (honorific language). German audiences expect formal "Sie" vs. informal "du" depending on context. French marketing increasingly uses "tu" for younger audiences but "vous" for B2B.

Why: Wrong formality level makes your brand seem either unprofessional or cold and distant.

Action: Define formality guidelines per language in your brand style guide
12

Brand voice is adapted, not just translated

A playful, casual brand voice in English may need to become warm but professional in Japanese or direct and efficient in German. Adapt the personality to what resonates in each culture while maintaining brand consistency.

Why: Brand voice that feels unnatural in a language undermines authenticity and engagement.

Action: Create market-specific brand voice guidelines with examples
13

Text expansion/contraction is accounted for

English to German expands ~30%. English to Finnish can expand up to 40%. English to Chinese/Japanese contracts significantly. UI elements, buttons, headlines, and layouts must accommodate these changes.

Why: Truncated text looks broken; excessive whitespace looks unfinished.

Action: Test all UI elements with longest expected translations
SEO Localization
14

Keywords are researched per language, not translated

Conduct fresh keyword research in each target language. Search behavior, term popularity, and intent vary dramatically. Use local keyword tools and native speaker input.

Why: Translated keywords often have zero search volume or wrong intent in the target language.

Action: Complete local keyword research before starting localization
15

Meta titles and descriptions are locally optimized

Write unique, keyword-optimized meta titles and descriptions for each language version. These should read naturally and include a compelling call to action relevant to the local audience.

Why: Meta elements directly impact click-through rates from search results.

Action: Write meta elements after keyword research, not as translations
16

URL slugs are transliterated or translated

Use readable, keyword-containing URL slugs in each language. For non-Latin scripts, decide between transliteration (romanized version) or native script based on your URL strategy and CMS capabilities.

Why: Localized URLs improve CTR and provide additional keyword signals.

Action: Define URL slug strategy per language in technical SEO guidelines
Testing & Quality Assurance
17

Native speaker review completed

Every localized piece must be reviewed by a native speaker of the target language who is also a resident of or deeply familiar with the target market. Diaspora speakers may miss current local trends and evolving language.

Why: Machine translation and non-native review miss nuances that readers immediately notice.

Action: Schedule native review as a mandatory step in your workflow
18

Functional testing in target locale completed

Test forms, CTAs, dynamic content, and interactive elements with locale-specific inputs. Verify that date pickers, address fields, and payment forms work correctly with local formats and input methods.

Why: Functional bugs in localized versions directly block conversions.

Action: Create locale-specific test cases for all interactive elements
19

Layout and rendering verified across devices

Check that translated content renders correctly on mobile and desktop. RTL languages (Arabic, Hebrew) need full layout mirroring. CJK text needs appropriate line-breaking and font rendering.

Why: Layout breaks are the most visible sign of a low-quality localization.

Action: Browser test each language version on mobile, tablet, and desktop
20

Back-translation or quality scoring completed

For critical content (legal, medical, financial), perform back-translation to verify accuracy. For marketing content, use a quality scoring framework such as MQM (Multidimensional Quality Metrics) to objectively assess localization quality.

Why: Systematic quality measurement identifies patterns and improves future localization.

Action: Implement quality scoring for ongoing localization quality tracking
